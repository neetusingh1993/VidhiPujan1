Annaprashan Pujan

Annaprashana/Onnoprashon/Annaprashana Vidhi/Annaprasan/Anna-Prasanam is an ancestral hindu samskara(ritual) which is performed when food other than milk is given to the new born

It is baby’s transition from liquid to solid foods. Annaprashana literally means ‘’food feeding’’ or ‘’first feeding of food’’ .

It is an important ceremony of grain initiation in modern India which is arranged in consultation with a priest who gives an auspicious date to conduct the Puja for the baby. It is usually done in six to eight months time of baby’s age. Even month for boy and odd month for girl.

A symbolic feeding of few spoons of rice pudding(kheer or payash) with the help of silver spoon is given to the baby. Silver metal is considered most positive for the body.

This Puja brings good health,a long life span and also cleanses any unwanted material which the baby may have swallowed in mother’s womb.

  • Every Puja samagri required for this puja.
  • Flowers and fruits.
  • Visarjan( immersion of remains of Puja in river, pond or any other local water body).

Bhoomi Pujan

Bhoomi Puja is performed as a customary practice before constructing any building on construction site. It is done with the help of a priest who gives an auspicious date for the puja.

This Puja is mainly done at the north-east corner of the construction site. This Puja is performed for Goddess Bhoomi, Vastu Purush and Panch bhootas(five elements of nature).

This Pooja should be done by the head of the family with wife to receive blessings from mother earth to get rid of all the evils and negativities from the land. It also purifies the construction site.

Brihspati Pujan

This Puja is the concluding ceremony after doing fasting(Vrat) for minimum of 7 Thursday and maximum of 108 Thursday. At the end of Vrat, Udyapan is done as a thanks giving to God.

Thursday is represented as Brihaspativaar or Vrihaspativaar. It is the day of Lord Vishnu and Brihaspati who is the guru of Vedas.

On the day of fasting, devotee wear yellow clothes, offers yellow bhog and Prasad to lord Vishnu and Brihaspati to gain job, health, wealth and happiness.

Benefits of the Puja:

  • Jupitar is the main planet after Sun in size and strongly placed Jupiter in horoscope makes the person truthful and honest and offers divine grace.
  • This puja is very helpful to married couples for a happy married life.
  • Light is symbolized with yellow rays and vrihaspati is considered sacred of all planets, therefore, Jupiter spread light and happiness among those who perform this puja.
  • Childless couples get the blessings in the form of child.

Engagement Puja

This Puja is done before marriage as a formal binding of couple together to receive blessings from gods and goddesses. It is also known as ring ceremony/ roka / chunni / sagai / nischitartham / mangni / kurmai etc.

This Puja emotionally binds the couple together in the presence of gods and goddesses to make them feel a sense of togetherness till the time of marriage.

This pre wedding ritual is done by inviting a priest who fixes the auspicious date and time for the puja based on the planetary movements.

Gandmool Pujan

Among the 27 nakshatras in the vedic astrology, those nakshatras which are ruled by ketu and mercury are known as Gandmool. Gandmool nakshatras are ashwini, ashlesha, magaha, jyestha, moola and revati nakshatra. Placement of moon in any of these nakshatras gives rise to gandmool doshas.

Degree of moon can cause damage to the baby, parents or some other relative.

This puja should be done around 27 days of the child’s birth or as soon as possible.

This Puja is also known as Sataisa Puja.

Benefits of this Puja:

  • This Puja is done to remove the ill effects of mool doshas from horoscope and pacify the presiding deities of the nakshatras through various processes.
  • This shanti puja alleviate sufferings due to inappropriate nakshatras.
  • Makes the baby healthy and successful in the life ahead of him/her.

Godh Bharai Pujan

Godh bharai/ shaad/ dohan jeevan/valakaappu/seemandham/seemantham Puja is done in odd month during pregnancy to bring good luck to pregnant mother.It is usually 7th or 9th month of the pregnancy when the considered to have entered a safe phase. The literal meaning of ‘Godh Bharai’ is ‘fill the lap’.

A priest is invited to chant mantras and perform religious rites for the safe arrival of the baby. Main deity is goddess lakshmi.

On this ceremony, mother’s lap is filled with fruits ,sarees ,jewellary ,sweets , rice etc and elders give blessings and advices to the mother for the welfare and protection of the child.

Also, women wishper in mother’s ears which goes by like ‘ a healthy, smiling and lively baby will occupy your lap.

Benefits of this Puja:

  • Blessings of good health is received for both mother and child.
  • Protects the baby from any unwanted mishap at the time of delivery.

Puja procedure : Swasti vachnam, Sankalp, Ganpati puja, Kalash puja, Rudra puja, Aarti and Prasad distribution, Visarjan

Grah Shanti Pujan

Planets with proper alignments bring good events and with wrong or misplaced alignments bring unfortunate events. Any misplacement can have influence on career, finances, relationship, love or marital life, health etc.

This puja appeases malefic planets and corrects the misalignment to improve positive energies of the person and family performing this puja.

It is also done before moving into a house and as a pre-wedding puja.

  • Gives protection from evil eyes.
  • Brings a proper balance in one’s life.

Grah Pravesh Pujan

Griha Pravesh puja is done before entering into a new house whether it is rented or self owned.It is a set of complex puja rituals and rites which is performed to bring peace and harmony into new house.

There are three types of Griha Pravesh puja which are Apoorva, Sapoorva and Dwandha. Apoorva is done for the newly construted house, Sapoorva is done for the already constructed house and Dwandha is done after reconstruction from fire, earthquake, flood etc.

It is one of the most important puja for the hindus. A priest is invited to determine the perfect date and time as per the astrological charts and muhurats for the maximum flow of energy during the puja.
